The Founder

Pedro Evangelista Llamas was born in Pagsanjan, Laguna on August
01, 1884. Both of his parents, Gabriel Llamas and Eleutera Evangelista were
born and raised in the same town too.

He studied in St. Joseph Catholic School in Hongkong for two years
and continued his education in the United States where he received his Bachelor of
Science in Pharmaceutical Chemistry from the University of Michigan.

At the young age of 28, he married Josefa Gomez Francia on August
26, 1912 and was blessed with nine children: Herminio, Guadalupe, Fidel,
Amor, Luis, Jose, Mario, Carlos, and Eva.

At 32, he was elected Municipal President of Pagsanjan for four years
(1916–1920). He declined to be re-elected to the position, even when the
townspeople wanted him to continue on, due to his health problems.

In 1923, he founded the Pagsanjan Academy to serve and augment
the lack of schools in his town. Later on, he realized that there was also a
need for secondary schools. Children from Pagsanjan went to Laguna High
School in Sta. Cruz, Rural High in Los Banos or in Manila to further their
schooling. However, no one went to Calamba, as there were no secondary
schools there. This thought bore fruit and thus, Laguna Institute was established in 1930.

The Laguna Institute flourished amidst the havoc and strife of World War II.
It closed down temporarily in 1945 and opened its doors once again in 1950.

Don Pedro Llamas died January 13, 1946 at the age of 61 due to a failing heart condition.
His demise was a great loss for his family and to the community. Nevertheless, his love for
the Filipinos of Laguna continued on with the legacy he left with his heirs; and that is of humble
servitude exemplified by providing quality education to the community. He envisioned educating
the community and equipping people to become leaders leading them into abundance.

Philosophy

Inspired by Dr. Jose Rizal’s educational principles, LCBA Education
upholds a balanced curriculum towards holistic development
for a better quality of life.

Vision

LCBA envisions to become an autonomous higher education institution
committed to producing holistically developed and globally
competitive lifelong learners.

Mission

LCBA commits itself to the total human development of the learners
through value-laden and competency-based education, policy-relevant
research, and sustainable community involvement.

Institution's Core Values

As LCBAian, employees and students should develop and possess the following values:

INTEGRITY
This means doing the right thing even when no one is looking.

POSITIVITY
This is finding the good in everything.

MINDFULNESS
This means respecting and accepting individual differences.

EXCELLENCE
This is exemplifying proper attitude, skill, and knowledge.

QUALITY OBJECTIVES

1. Promote total development among students for better quality of life;

2. Employ experientially and academically aligned educators;

3. Implement values-laden and competency-based education;

4. Conduct policy-relevant personnel and student research projects;

5. Acquire adequate library holdings and maximize library utilization among faculty and students;

6. Provide curricular and co-curricular activities delivered by highly competent and service-oriented personnel;

7. Provide state-of-the-art laboratories and facilities;

8. Provide a satisfactory, conducive, and safe learning environment;

9. Develop and utilize efficient and effective administrative systems;

10. Implement community-empowering extension program; and

11. Produce globally competitive graduates who are driven to pursue further studies and are equipped for the world of employment and entrepreneurship.
